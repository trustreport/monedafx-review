Trading Performance Analysis

Moneda FX provides trading performance data on platforms like Myfxbook and FX Blue. One notable strategy, "Quant," reports an absolute gain of +27.18% and a gain of +96.29%. The disparity between these figures warrants scrutiny.

Understanding the Gain Discrepancy

  • Absolute Gain (+27.18%): Reflects the growth of the initial deposit, excluding factors like withdrawals, additional deposits, or compounding.
  • Gain (+96.29%): Accounts for all profits relative to the current balance, incorporating compounding and any account activity.

The significant difference between these metrics can be attributed to:

  • Large Deposits Post-Initiation: Adding substantial funds after trading begins can inflate the Gain percentage without a corresponding rise in Absolute Gain.
  • Withdrawals and Potential Manipulation: Significant withdrawals can distort Gain calculations, a tactic sometimes employed to present misleadingly high performance figures.
  • High-Risk Trading Strategies: Techniques like martingale or grid strategies may temporarily recover losses but pose substantial risk, potentially leading to account depletion during market downturns.
  • Data Integrity Concerns: Without independent verification, there's a possibility of data manipulation, especially if the broker restricts access to detailed account information.

Drawdown Considerations

The reported drawdown for the "Quant" strategy is 17.48%, indicating a notable peak-to-trough decline. While not catastrophic, such a drawdown is significant and may be concerning for investors seeking low-risk opportunities.
